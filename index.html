Stroller + Car Seat

This might be one of the most expensive items you'll purchase before your newborn arrives. But it's also the item that will frustrate you the most if you don't make the right purchase.

As one new mother put it to us, "Don’t fuck around with the stroller. Buy a goodie. I tried to go cheap and ended up returning it. Something that's easy to clean, and rolls like butter with 1 hand. I know this sounds ridiculous, but when you’re holding your iced coffee, answering emails, and wheeling the little pork chop along — it’s essential."

Affordable option

Chicco Bravo Trio Travel System

If you're looking for an affordable stroller and car seat, and one that keeps your kiddo safe, this Amazon review right here will tell you all you need to know:

"This was the best money I ever spent. My granddaughter (3 weeks old, 8lbs) was just involved in a car accident. It was a head on collision with all the airbags deployed and both cars totaled. 4 Adults between the two cars all injured with various broken bones. The man in the back seat in the same car sitting right next to my granddaughter was airlifted to shock trauma with a broken back. All the adults were wearing seat belts. But you can imagine the jolt/shock. Ava did not have a scratch. The car Chicco Bravo car seat with the base locking system absorbed the impact and protected her. No doubt this seat with all the safety systems, harness, infant liner, etc. saved her life. I hate to think what would have happened in a cheap seat."

We test drove the Chicco Bravo about 10 times, and really liked it, primarily because we felt so confident in the Keyfit30 car seat and base, and because we'd heard great things from our friends. We were all set to buy it, but decided to spend a little extra money and splurged on the UPPAbaby stroller.

Posh Option

UPPAbaby Vista + Nuna Car Seat

We spent months looking at strollers, and we kinda hated all of them. We were all set to buy the Chicco Bravo, but ended up purchasing the UPPAbaby because it glided along with hardly any muscling, and it folded and expanded so easily that we just did that over and over again in the store — it felt that good. Seriously, the salespeople kept coming up to us and asking if there was a problem, and we said "Nope", and just went right back to folding and expanding the stroller.

For the car seat, you could choose to buy the UPPAbaby Mesa Car Seat, and it looks and feels like a really good car seat. But, when you start using the Nuna Pipa, it will be easy to handle because it's so light (7.9 pounds), and you'll fall in love with the little details — like the magnetic sun guard that pulls out and clings to the sides of the car seat with so very little effort. The only hassle is that you'll need the UPPAbaby VISTA Upper Adapter for the Nuna car seat.

Baby Carrier

You don't have to buy a baby carrier, but you'll be so glad that you did. You can go about your day, doing your normal business, while the baby is right there connected to you. You know — make dinner, buy groceries, do the laundry — all without having to lose sight or worry about your little pork chop.

Don't bother shopping around — the Baby Bjorn is the only carrier you're going to need to try. That's what all of our friends told us. And now we'll tell you the same thing.

Crib + Toddler Bed

We looked everywhere to try to find the right crib. Ikea's cribs were too flimsy and just looked and felt cheap. Crate and Barrel's cribs were really nice, but so expensive that we couldn't justify the price. Other cribs were either too bulky, or or too flimsy. When we found Babyletto Crib, we quickly found that this was the right crib for us. Sturdy, good looking, and it hit the right price point.

If you buy a co-sleeping unit, you could wait until baby's 3rd month to buy and put together a crib.

Newborn Diapers

Professionals recommend making sure you have 100 newborn diapers for the first week, and you'll go through 10 or more diapers per day. The newborn diapers can be used until your baby reaches around 10 pounds, at which time you'll need to upgrade to the next diaper size.

Books

For her

Mindful Birthing, by Nancy Bardacke

—

For him

The Expectant Father: The Ultimate Guide for Dads-to-Be, by Armin A. Brott and Jennifer Ash

There's nothing like reading aloud the chapters describing how your expecting wife will be acting in the current month to your wife, and watching her eyes grow wide realizing the description has described her to a tee. The other books for guys were rubbish, and this is the only book that I found was worth reading cover to cover.

Apps

For her

The Bump + Ovia

Both apps were helpful for reading timely articles related to your stage of pregnancy.

The Bump: iTunes, Android

Ovia: iTunes, Android

For him

None

People recommended a contraction counting app, but we found them completely unhelpful. When a contraction starts, it takes 15-20 seconds to realize a contraction has started, to find your phone, open the app, and start the timer.

I wish I'd have been more present with my wife while she was working through a contraction, and that I'd worn a watch with a seconds hand, and logged the contraction times with pen and paper.
