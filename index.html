Baby Monitor

You'll want a baby monitor to keep an ear out for your baby's cries, and you might even want a camera to keep an eye out as well. Audio monitors are great for listening when you're a few rooms away. Meanwhile, video monitors are ideal for when you're out of the house and want to keep an eye on your baby, or to make sure your caretaker is treating your little one properly.

We love the Vtech Audio Baby Monitor, and the 2 parent units allow us to keep an ear out as we move from room to room without having to carry a device with us.

For video monitors, the Nest security cam is perfect. Easily mount the camera above the crib or anywhere in the nursery, and then configure the device so that you can receive alerts, and login to watch your little one from anywhere via your smart phone. And after your tot is grown up, you can convert the camera into a security device that you can repurpose anywhere in your home.

Also, take it from us - don't buy any baby monitors produced by AngelCare. They're crap.

Humidifier

You'll want to maintain a humidity level of between 35-50% in the room where the baby sleeps. This will be necessary in dry climates, and in the winter in cold climates.

Recommended Humidifier

Safety 1st 360 Cool Mist Ultrasonic Humidifier

Humidity Monitor + Thermometer

You'll want a thermometer to monitor the temperature, and a hygrometer to measure the humidity levels in your nursery.

Experts recommend a temperature between 68-72°F in the room where the baby sleeps. Higher temperatures are thought to be a cause of SIDS (Sudden Infant Death Syndrome) in children under 1 year of age.

Experts also recommend a humidity level of between 35-50% in the room where the baby sleeps. If the humidity is below 35%, use a humidifier to increase the humidity in the room.

Co-sleeping unit

Having a co-sleeping crib attached to your bed is amazing for the first month, because it means you won't need to get up every time you need to check on your baby. We bought the Chicco co-sleeping crib, but don't recommend it, because it's too low for a standard American bed.

Baby Carrier

You don't have to buy a baby carrier, but you'll be so glad that you did. You can go about your day, doing your normal business, while the baby is right there connected to you. You know — make dinner, buy groceries, do the laundry — all without having to lose sight or worry about your little pork chop. One thing to keep in mind is that babies usually need to reach a minimum weight before they can be placed in the carrier.

Don't bother shopping around — the Baby Bjorn is the only carrier you're going to need to try. That's what all of our friends told us. And now we'll tell you the same thing.
